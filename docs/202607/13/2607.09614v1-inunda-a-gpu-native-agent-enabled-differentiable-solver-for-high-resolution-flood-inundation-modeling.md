## 摘要
预测洪水流向和深度，在高分辨率和大范围区域中，传统水力求解器计算成本高昂，而纯数据驱动替代方法虽然快速但缺乏物理保证，且泛化能力差。我们提出了Inunda，一种原生GPU的洪水淹没模型，用于求解二维浅水方程。Inunda采用质量守恒局部惯性格式，在单个GPU上数分钟内可模拟覆盖数百万个网格单元的多日事件。由于所有算子都兼容自动微分，该求解器天生可微分：模型参数可通过反向模式自动微分对全模拟过程进行梯度下降估计，以拟合观测数据。我们在三个案例中展示了Inunda。针对德克萨斯州哈里斯县2017年飓风哈维的后报，Inunda与调查高水位的平均绝对误差为0.67米，与一系列现有洪水模型相比具有竞争力或更优，并且达到了中位水位纳什效率系数+0.72，是运营中的国家水模型v3.0的+0.31的两倍多。针对2025年7月德克萨斯中部的山洪，Inunda由18个成员1公里对流允许降水集合驱动，生成概率洪水预报，其技能随着洪峰前导时间缩短而系统性提高。在里奥鲁伊多索火烧迹地的火灾后山洪应用中，可微分校准恢复了饱和水力传导率作为模型自身分辨率的空间显式场，并追踪了其多年火灾后恢复过程。Inunda为真实事件洪水建模提供了开放、端到端的管道，将基于物理的水力学的准确性与现代可微编程的校准和耦合优势相结合。

## Abstract
Predicting where floodwater goes and how deep it gets, at high resolution and across large domains, remains computationally expensive with conventional hydraulic solvers, while purely data-driven surrogates are fast but lack physical guarantees and generalize poorly beyond their training events. We present Inunda, a GPU-native flood inundation model that solves the two-dimensional shallow water equations. Inunda uses a mass-conservative local-inertial scheme and runs multi-day events over millions of cells in minutes on a single GPU. Because every operator is autograd-compatible, the solver is differentiable by construction: model parameters can be estimated by gradient descent against gage observations through reverse-mode automatic differentiation of the full simulation. We demonstrate Inunda on three case studies. For a hindcast of Hurricane Harvey (2017) in Harris County, Texas, Inunda matches surveyed high-water marks to a mean absolute error of 0.67 m, competitive with or better than a suite of established flood models, and reaches a median gage water-level Nash Sutcliffe efficiency of +0.72, more than double the +0.31 of the operational National Water Model v3.0. For the July 2025 Central Texas flash flood, Inunda is driven by an 18-member 1-km convection-allowing precipitation ensemble to produce probabilistic flood forecasts whose skill improves systematically as lead time to the crest shortens. For a post-fire flash-flood application in the Rio Ruidoso burn scar, differentiable calibration recovers the saturated hydraulic conductivity as a spatially explicit field at the model's own resolution and traces its multi-year post-fire recovery. Inunda provides an open, end-to-end pipeline for real-event flood modeling that couples the accuracy of physics-based hydraulics with the calibration and coupling advantages of modern differentiable programming.
